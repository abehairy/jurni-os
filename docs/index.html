The problem

You already write the book of you, every day. You just can't read it.

Fragmented

Claude knows what you're building. ChatGPT knows what you're learning. X knows what you're arguing about. Your calendar knows who's pulling on your time. Your photos remember what you saw. Each holds a fragment. Nothing holds the whole.

Rented

And that fragment lives on someone else's servers, under someone else's terms. The richest record of your inner life — the one you generated — isn't yours to search, to read back, or to own.

Jurni inverts that. Local by default. Open source. Your key, your machine, your data.

What you get

One quiet place for the whole of you.

Life Landscape

A living treemap of the topics, projects and people you return to most. Zoom in, zoom out, watch it shift over weeks.

People you talk about

Not a follower count. The humans you actually mention — in your private threads, weighted by how you bring them up.

Weekly briefing

Every Monday, a one-page read-out of what shifted, what you focused on, what's repeating, what's new.

How it works

Three pieces. Nothing leaves your machine.

01
Connect
Point Jurni at Claude, ChatGPT, X, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, your calendar, your photos. Connectors run in a local browser; no OAuth handoffs to our servers, because there are no servers.
02
Process
Each new conversation, post or event is broken into moments. An LLM you provide (OpenRouter, Anthropic, OpenAI — your key) extracts entities, emotions, themes, decisions. Everything lands in a local SQLite database under ~/.jurni/.
03
Reflect
The dashboard reads that database directly. Every view — Landscape, People, Timeline, Briefing — is a query, not a cloud call. Close the app and it all stays on your disk, readable with any SQLite client.

Privacy
Your life's record should live on your machine, under your terms.
NO CLOUD · NO ACCOUNT · NO TELEMETRY · NO DARK PATTERNS

Your key, your calls

Jurni never embeds a managed AI key. You bring your own OpenRouter (or Anthropic, or OpenAI) key the first time you launch. Every inference is a direct call from your machine to the model. We don't proxy, log, or even see the traffic.

Readable, portable, yours

The whole database is a single SQLite file in ~/.jurni/. Back it up with Time Machine. Inspect it with any SQLite browser. Delete it any time and Jurni is gone — no orphan accounts, no servers holding your shadow.

Read back your year, on your machine.
