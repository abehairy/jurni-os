What it shows you

Three things, nothing made up.

Jurni doesn't generate a score or tell you what to do. It pulls out what's already in your conversations and lets you slide week by week to see how it's changed.

01
Topics

What you actually talk about.

The real subjects from your own conversations — projects, people, places, questions. Whatever yours are. Bigger tile means more of that week went to it.

02
Domains

Where in your life it sits.

Each topic is tagged work, money, family, body, and so on — twelve categories in total. Color-coded, so at a glance you can see if your week was mostly work or mostly not.

03
Tones

How the week felt, in a word.

Each tile gets a single word read from your own language: open, stuck, alive, waiting, warm, unsettled. Usually accurate. Sometimes uncomfortably so.

How it works

Three steps. All of them on your Mac.

01
Connect

Log in once, in a local window.

Jurni opens a small browser on your Mac and you log into Claude, ChatGPT, X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook — whichever ones you use. No OAuth through our servers. We don't have any.

02
Process

Your AI key, on your machine.

Paste an OpenRouter, Anthropic, or OpenAI key the first time you launch. Each conversation is broken up and analyzed with your key, direct to the model. Results get written to a SQLite file at ~/.jurni/. We never see any of it.

03
Read

Slide the scrubber. Click a tile.

Watch the picture redraw week by week. Click into a tile to see the actual threads behind it, the people named, the tone. Quit the app and everything stays on disk, in a plain SQLite file you own.

Privacy
No cloud. No account. No telemetry. Your key, your machine, your data.
One SQLite file at ~/.jurni/. Back it up with Time Machine. Delete it and Jurni is gone.

Try it now. It's free.

Open source, runs entirely on your Mac. Signed and notarized by Apple, so it just opens — no Gatekeeper warning, no right-click trick. Updates install silently from here on.
