What you're actually looking at

Three axes. Nothing invented.

Jurni doesn't generate a score or a prescription. It names what's already in your conversations, and lets you slide week by week through how it's shifted.

01
Topics

The named things you return to.

Not tags. Not keywords. The actual subjects — Clinera, Nesma, Career shift, Tokyo. Tile size = how much of your attention that period. Tile color = which domain it belongs to.

02
Domains

Twelve places a life gets lived.

Every topic is sorted into one of twelve domains. Red-browns for pressure and output, greens for growth, blues for contemplation, greys for the quiet parts. The palette does half the work of the insight.

03
Tones

One word for how the week felt.

Each tile carries a tone read from your own language. Open, stuck, alive, waiting, warm, unsettled. You'll recognize them before you finish reading.

How it works

Three moves. All of them local.

01
Connect

Point it at your chats.

A local browser connector walks through your Claude and ChatGPT history the same way you would. No OAuth, no server on our side — we don't have one.

02
Process

Categorize with your own LLM key.

Each conversation is broken into moments. An LLM you provide (OpenRouter, Anthropic, OpenAI) extracts topic, domain, people, and tone. Results land in a SQLite file in ~/.jurni/. Nothing transits us.

03
Read

Slide week by week.

The landscape redraws in place. Drill into a tile for the specific threads behind it — the stories, the people named, the tone reading. Quit the app and everything stays on your disk, readable by any SQLite client.

Privacy — the actual commitment
No cloud. No account. No telemetry. Your key, your machine, your data.
Single SQLite file at ~/.jurni/ · back it up with Time Machine · delete and it's gone
